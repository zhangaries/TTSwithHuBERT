Audio samples from "Improving Text-to-Speech Synthesis with Self-Supervised Representationn"

Authors: Haoyu Zhang, Haopeng Lin, Shichao Liu, Chunfeng Wang, Yuxiang Zou, Yahuan Cong, Chuxiong Zhang, Junhui Zhang, Xiang Yin, Zejun Ma.

Abstract: Nowadays, end-to-end Text-to-Speech (TTS) has shown sub- stantial improvement in naturalness, but still suffers from the demand of a huge amount of high quality speech recordings and the difficulty in disentanglement. Meanwhile, self-supervised learning has shown great potential for learning information from unlabelled data. Recent researches in speech domain also show that self-supervised learning has the ability of represen- tation disentanglement in voice conversion. In this study, we explore to use self-supervised speech representation learning feature in TTS tasks, trying to improve voice quality and dis- entangle timbre information. By utilizing HuBERT representa- tion, our model first learns the relationship between phone se- quences from different languages and the unified pronunciation embedding space, then it recovers timbre information by map- ping the HuBERT space to mel spectrogram. The audios are finally generated by a neutral vocoder. Our experiments show that we build a pretty good TTS system, which outperforms sig- nificantly in speech quality and cross-lingual transfer. To the best of our knowledge, we are the first to apply Self-Supervised Learning representation directly on TTS.
